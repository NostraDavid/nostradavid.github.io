World of Warcraft Starter Edition Guide

aka trial/f2p/free to play edition

Note: This guide is written so people who are new to World of Warcraft/MMOs can be eased into Retail for both newbies to World of Warcraft in general and for MMO newbies.

So you want to try out World of Warcraft, but haven't played Warcraft since The Frozen Throne came out in 2003? Then you're on the right track to become the best Level 20 toon there ever was!

Zones & Spawn points

The world of Warcraft is cut up into zones. The enemies of each zone are set to a specific level. Hover on your in-game world map to see which zone is made for each level. Starter zones (where your toon spawns) are always set to 1-10

Limitations

It would not be a trail version if it did not have some restrictions. Here are the ones I've walked into during my playthroughs:

  • Level limit of 20
  • Gold limit of 10
  • No pet battles
  • No adding strangers as friends
  • No auction house
    • MAKE SURE THE ABOVE IS TRUE

Note: This is NOT an extensive list. Just the things I have run into

Level limit

This means your movement is restricted to certain zones. Any zone whose minimal level goes several levals above yours means you will miss some 95% of your hits and thus won't be able to do any damage before running out of materials too boost you enough to kill even a single enemy

You also won't be able to gain all abilities, as you gain those per several levels. No worries though, by the time you are level 20, you will have enough abilities to have fun with.

Gold limit

Minor spoiler: You will be able to buy a mount, but only the basic ones. Anything beyond that will mean you can't buy it, simply because you can't hold enough gold.

You can somewhat work around this limit by buying ethereal threads MAKE SURE THIS IS TRUE, which can stack up to 200, which means you can use it as a gold replacement. Kind of annoying since you lose a lot of money, but it's better than nothing.

Pet battles

You may notice small critters running around (bunnies and the like). You can kill them (which I sometimes do out of frustration, because no pet battles ;_;), but when you click on them, you'll get a notification that you can not do any pet battles.

No friends

can't add friends IIRC.

Auction house

Simply not available, which means you can't sell or buy things there

Settings

World of Warcraft has quite a few non-standard options for games in general getting used to them is a bit of a bitch, so here's a guide on what does what

Check out and make screenshots of menus

You can point and click, like an RTS (NOT RECOMMENDED - use the autorun option instead)

Enable all the menubars, so you can fill the shit out of them. You will not use what you can not see.

Enable auto-looting - you can always throw away the things you don't use (or don't cost anything)

Shortcuts

World of Warcraft has a ton of menus and submenus and finding those is a total bitch to do.

Remembering shortcuts is (IMO) much easier.
